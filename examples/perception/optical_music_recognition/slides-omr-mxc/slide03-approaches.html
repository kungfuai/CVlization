Four Approaches to OMR

1. Rule-based (Audiveris)

Classical OMR engine. Failed on vintage scan: spurious key changes, sparse notes, wrong beat counts. Works on clean modern prints only.

2. OMR Transformer (SMT)

Trained on GrandStaff dataset. 4% CER on clean crops. Degrades badly on vintage: note collisions, missing staves. Needs system-level crops, not full pages.

3. Zero-shot VLMs (Frontier APIs)

Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Opus 4.6, GPT-5.2 Pro. Gemini produced the best output. All disagree on key signature. Useful for metadata, not note-level transcription.

4. Fine-tuned VLMs (This work)

SFT on open-source VLMs with MXC format. First approach to produce pitched notes from page images. Best result: 35% pitch similarity with Qwen3.5-9B.
