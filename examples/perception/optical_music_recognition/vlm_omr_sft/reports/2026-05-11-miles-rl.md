# Miles RL Experiments for OMR

**Previous**: [`2026-05-05-rl-experiments.md`](2026-05-05-rl-experiments.md) — TRL/unsloth GRPO findings.

## Why try Miles

After 6 TRL GRPO runs (v1-v6b), we ran out of obvious knobs in the
unsloth/TRL framework. Best result was maintaining the 84% SFT
baseline on Level 9 — no improvement. Miles offers:

1. **Parallel rollouts** via SGLang (separate process). TRL was sequential.
2. **True on-policy training** — bit-wise identical log-probs between
   training and inference. TRL's vLLM integration is approximate.
3. **Full fine-tuning** instead of LoRA r=32. Removes the capacity ceiling.
4. **First-class VLM support** — validated for Qwen3-VL family.
5. **Both GPUs** can be used (TRL runs single-GPU in our setup).

## Setup

- **Model**: Qwen3.5-9B VLM (merged from SFT LoRA, `outputs/tamqjf4k_merged`)
- **Data**: Level 9 synthetic, 200 samples
- **Reward**: SequenceMatcher.ratio() on MXC2 pitches (same as eval metric)
- **Backend**: FSDP, colocate mode, 2 GPUs
- **Code**: `examples/perception/optical_music_recognition/vlm_omr_miles/`

## Inference speed

Text-only generation in startup test was ~0.7s/token (with cuda_graph disabled).
Production rate with cuda_graph + batching should be 10-50× faster.
Will benchmark during the first training run.

## Metrics already in Miles

Miles tracks via WandB/MLflow:
- `rollout/entropy` — mode collapse detection
- `train/kl_loss`, `train/ppo_kl` — policy drift
- `train/pg_clipfrac` — update aggressiveness
- `raw_response_length/{mean,max,min}` — length distribution
