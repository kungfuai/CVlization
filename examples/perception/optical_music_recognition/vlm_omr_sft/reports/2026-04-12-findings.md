## Findings (2026-04-12)

### What is reliably established

These findings are based on properly sized evaluations (n≥50) or consistent
results across multiple independent experiments.

1. **MXC format is necessary.** 0% pitch accuracy with XML targets across all
   6 models tested. XML tokens are consumed by preamble before reaching notes.
   This is replicated across every model and training configuration.

2. **Models under 8B cannot learn pitch.** Four different 3-4B architectures
   (Ministral-3, Gemma-3 4B, DeepSeek-OCR-2) all score 0-1% pitch. This is
   consistent across LoRA ranks (r=8, r=32) and training durations (2-4 epochs).

3. **Architecture matters more than scale.** Qwen3.5-9B (9.5B params) outperforms
   Qwen3-VL-32B (32B params) — 35% vs 23% pitched-only similarity. Both measured
   on n=10, but the gap is large enough to be directionally reliable.

4. **LoRA r=32 is optimal for Qwen3.5-9B.** r=16 (33%), r=32 (36%), r=64 (32%)
   on n=10. r=64 overfits without improving. The rank sweep was done on the same
   data with identical settings.

5. **Training target truncation was a major bottleneck.** Level 7 MXC is ~4200
   tokens. At `max_length=4096`, the training target and EOS token were
   truncated. Increasing to 8192 improved pitch from 47% → 67% (+20pp).
   EOS is correctly learned on Levels 1-3 (where targets fit within 4096)
   but not learned when targets are truncated.

6. ~~**The voice part is the dominant accuracy factor (−55pp).**~~ **RETRACTED.**
   This claim from Experiment 13 was based on Level 6b (30-36%) vs Level 7a
   (91%), but Experiment 14 showed 6b's low score was caused by training
   instability, not content difficulty. See Experiment 14.

7. ~~**Lyrics improve accuracy (+17pp).**~~ **PENDING RE-VERIFICATION.**
   The 7a → 7b comparison (42% → 59% on old ablation runs) is potentially
   also affected by training instability. Needs re-run with stable training.

8. **Level 6 (16m) reliably scores 95% with either 4K or 8K context.** The
   8K control experiment (Experiment 14) reproduced the original Level 6
   result with n=50 validation. Qwen3.5-9B + 8K context is not the problem.

9. **Training instability at step 811 is deterministic.** Both 3-epoch and
   5-epoch 6b runs collapsed at exactly the same step with seed=3407. The
   3-epoch run never recovered; the 5-epoch run oscillated between good and
   catastrophic states before stabilizing. Root cause: a specific training
   batch at ~epoch 0.8 triggers neural text degeneration (repetition loops).

8. **More synthetic data lowers eval_loss but does not improve pitch.** 1K → 5K
   samples: eval_loss improved 5× (0.002 → 0.0004), but pitch stayed ~47% on
   n=50. The model learns better syntax, not better pitch reading.

9. **Inference speed: ~26 tok/s, ~2.5 min/sample for Level 7.** Consistent
   across runs on Blackwell RTX PRO 6000. Flash-attn 2.8.3 is incompatible
   with this GPU (produces garbage output on SM120).

**Key observations:**

1. **Level 6 (16m) + 8K context scores 95% on n=50** — matches the original
   4K run exactly. Context length is not the issue.

2. **Level 6b (24m) scores are unreliable due to training instability.**
   Both 3-epoch and 5-epoch runs collapsed at step 811 (~epoch 0.8) and
   produced degenerate predictions with 127-274× repetition of single
   4-grams. See Experiment 14. We do not know the "true" 6b accuracy.

3. **The voice part hypothesis (from Exp 13) is retracted.** Without stable
   6b training, we cannot isolate the voice-part effect from length effect.

4. **OLiMPiC (39%) and openscore (35%) are limited by different factors than
   synthetic data.** Both are real music with irregular rhythms, dense
   voicings, and other features the synthetic ladder doesn't capture.

5. **eval_loss is misleading** — it tracks format/syntax memorization, not
   actual transcription accuracy. Can coexist with degenerate generation.

### Length is the dominant factor (after fixing training instability)

The 16-measure → 24-measure jump costs ~25-30pp in pitch accuracy:

| | 16 measures | 24 measures | Length penalty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Piano only | 95% (Level 6) | 68% (Level 6b stable) | −27pp |
| Voice + piano + lyrics | 91% (Level 7a) | 67% (Level 7) | −24pp |

The voice part itself has a small effect (~1-4pp at the same length).
The earlier "voice part is dominant (−55pp)" claim was an artifact of
training instability on the 6b run.

### Loss spikes scale with output sequence length (verified)

| Run | Output tokens | Spikes >5× | Max ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| 16-measure runs (Level 6, 7a, 7b, 7c) | ~2000-2700 | 0-2 | up to 12× |
| 24-measure runs (Level 6b, 7) | ~3000-4200 | 9-16 | up to 88× |

This is a known issue in long-sequence LLM fine-tuning — see "Spike No
More" (COLM 2025) and "Sequence Length Warmup" (Li et al.). Our current
mitigation (lower LR + tighter clipping) helped Level 6b reach 68%, but
spikes still occur. The most promising untried fix is **Sequence Length
Warmup**: train on 16m first, then fine-tune on 24m.

### Open questions

1. **Would Sequence Length Warmup eliminate the 24m instability?** Train
   on Level 6 (16m) first to convergence, then continue training on Level
   6b (24m) with the warmed-up adapter. Should be much more stable.

2. **What is the true openscore accuracy with 8K context?** The 35% number
   used 4K context (heavy truncation on 10K+ token pages). Retraining with
   8K or 16K context could significantly improve real-music accuracy.

3. **Why is EOS learning still partial on stable 6b?** The model uses
   `<|endoftext|>` (token 248044) instead of the trained `<|im_end|>`
   (248046). Is this an unsloth chat template issue, or does the model
   need more training to settle on the right stop token?

4. **Can we close the synthetic-to-real gap?** OLiMPiC (39%) and openscore
   (35%) are both n=10 estimates. With the stable training setup and 8K
   context, the real numbers might be significantly higher.

## Next steps

### 1. Key signature training

The accidental errors suggest the model needs more examples of each key signature.
Current Level 3 has random keys (-4 to +4) across 800 samples = ~100 per key.
Generating more samples or oversampling rare keys may help.

### 2. RL with musical correctness rewards

Use structural rewards (valid MXC, measure durations, pitch range) to push
past the SFT ceiling. Best attempted after diagnostic runs stabilize.

### 3. Investigate bimodal accuracy distribution

The 50-sample eval shows 46% of samples >50% pitch and 26% near 0%.
Understanding what distinguishes success vs failure cases would guide
data augmentation or curriculum design.

### 4. vLLM integration for faster eval

vLLM v0.17.0 has native Qwen3.5 support with optimized linear attention
kernels. Once tokenizer compatibility is resolved, this could enable
batched inference for 2-4× faster evaluation.
